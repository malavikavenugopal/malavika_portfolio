Market Segmentation

Course: Introduction to Business Analytics

Institution: Amrita School of Business, Coimbatore

Tutor: Dr. Prashobhan Palakkeel

Introduction

Market segmentation is a crucial marketing strategy that divides a broad target market into subsets of consumers who have common needs, preferences, or characteristics. It allows businesses to tailor their products, services, and marketing efforts to specific groups, enhancing customer satisfaction and increasing marketing efficiency.

In the context of business analytics, market segmentation uses data analysis techniques to identify these distinct groups based on demographic, geographic, psychographic, and behavioral factors.

Importance of Market Segmentation

  • Enables targeted marketing campaigns and personalized messaging.
  • Improves product development by focusing on specific customer needs.
  • Helps in allocating marketing resources more efficiently.
  • Enhances competitive advantage by understanding customer groups better.

Types of Market Segmentation

  1. Demographic Segmentation: Age, gender, income, education, etc.
  2. Geographic Segmentation: Location, climate, urban/rural, etc.
  3. Psychographic Segmentation: Lifestyle, personality, values.
  4. Behavioral Segmentation: Purchasing habits, brand loyalty, usage rate.

Conclusion

Effective market segmentation helps businesses to understand their customers deeply and make informed decisions based on data-driven insights. This leads to more successful marketing strategies and business growth.
